What is Digital Literacy?

Digital Literacy is the ability to use digital technology, communication tools, and networks effectively and responsibly. It encompasses finding, evaluating, creating, and communicating information using digital platforms - an essential skill for academic excellence, career success, and safe online navigation.

🌐 Safe Internet Practices

  • Use strong, unique passwords for each account
  • Enable two-factor authentication (2FA)
  • Verify URLs before clicking links
  • Avoid public Wi-Fi for sensitive transactions
  • Keep software and browsers updated
  • Be cautious with personal information sharing

💼 Professional Online Presence

  • Create a complete LinkedIn profile with clear photo
  • Build a GitHub portfolio showcasing projects
  • Use professional email addresses
  • Maintain consistent branding across platforms
  • Share relevant achievements and certifications
  • Network with peers and industry professionals

📧 Email Etiquette

  • Use clear, descriptive subject lines
  • Start with appropriate greetings (Dear/Hello)
  • Keep messages concise and professional
  • Proofread before sending
  • Use proper sign-offs with full name
  • Reply within 24-48 hours
  • Avoid informal language and emojis

🛡️ Cybersecurity Awareness

  • Recognize phishing emails and fake websites
  • Never share OTPs or passwords with anyone
  • Verify UPI requests before payment
  • Report suspicious activity (Helpline: 1930)
  • Backup important data regularly
  • Think before you click or share
