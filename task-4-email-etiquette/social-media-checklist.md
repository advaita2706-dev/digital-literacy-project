# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students

A responsible digital citizen checklist for B.Tech students.

---

## ✅ DO's

| # | Do This |
|---|---------|
| 1 | **Keep your profiles professional** – Use a clear, appropriate photo and bio on LinkedIn and other platforms that employers might view. |
| 2 | **Think before you post** – Ask yourself: "Would I be comfortable if my professor or future employer saw this?" |
| 3 | **Use privacy settings** – Regularly review who can see your posts, photos, and personal information. |
| 4 | **Give credit for shared content** – Always acknowledge the original creator when sharing someone else's work, ideas, or images. |
| 5 | **Report harmful content** – If you encounter cyberbullying, misinformation, or illegal content, report it to the platform. |
| 6 | **Keep your accounts secure** – Enable two-factor authentication on all social media accounts. |
| 7 | **Separate personal and professional accounts** – Maintain distinct personas for social and professional networking. |

---

## ❌ DON'Ts

| # | Avoid This |
|---|-----------|
| 1 | **Don't share personal sensitive information** – Never post your phone number, address, Aadhaar number, or bank details publicly. |
| 2 | **Don't spread unverified news** – Always verify information from at least two reliable sources before sharing. |
| 3 | **Don't engage in online arguments** – Heated online exchanges can damage your reputation and escalate into cyberbullying. |
| 4 | **Don't use social media as a venting platform** – Posting complaints about professors, classmates, or institutions can have serious academic consequences. |
| 5 | **Don't accept friend or follow requests from strangers** – Unknown connections can be phishing attempts or social engineering attacks. |
| 6 | **Don't click on suspicious links** – Even if shared by someone you know, verify before clicking – their account may have been compromised. |

---

*This checklist was prepared as part of the CSE0001 Digital Literacy project at VIT Bhopal University.*
