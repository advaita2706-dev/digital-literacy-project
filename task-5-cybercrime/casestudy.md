# Task 5 – Cybercrime Case Study

## Topic: UPI / Online Payment Fraud

### Overview

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is one of the fastest-growing cybercrimes in India, with the National Cyber Crime Portal reporting tens of thousands of cases annually. It exploits the widespread adoption of UPI apps such as PhonePe, Google Pay, and Paytm among college students and young adults.

### How It Typically Happens (Step-by-Step)

1. **Initial Contact:** The victim receives a call, text, or social media message from a stranger claiming to be a buyer (e.g., on OLX or a college resale group), a bank official, or a refund-processing agent.
2. **Building Trust:** The fraudster creates urgency or uses authoritative language — "I am from your bank's fraud team," or "I am sending you ₹5,000 for your old laptop."
3. **The Deception:** The fraudster sends a **collect request** (not a payment) via a UPI app, convincing the victim that it is a "payment incoming." They may ask the victim to enter their UPI PIN to "accept" the money.
4. **Money Lost:** The moment the victim enters their UPI PIN on a collect request, money is debited from their account — not credited. The fraudster has set up the request in reverse.
5. **Disappearance:** The fraudster disconnects and becomes unreachable. The victim realises the deception only after checking their bank balance.

### Who Is Usually Targeted

Young adults aged 18–30, first-time UPI users, college students selling items online, and people responding to "too-good-to-be-true" job offers or discount schemes.

### Consequences

Financial loss ranging from ₹500 to several lakhs. In many cases, the money is transferred immediately through layered accounts, making recovery difficult. Victims also experience emotional distress, anxiety, and loss of trust in digital payment systems.

**Source:** National Cyber Crime Portal, India (cybercrime.gov.in); RBI Digital Payments Report 2024.
