# Stay Safe Online – Prevention Checklist

### For College Students in India

---

| # | Prevention Tip |
|---|---------------|
| 1 | **Never enter your UPI PIN to "receive" money** – A PIN is only required when you are sending money. Any request asking you to enter your PIN to accept payment is a scam. |
| 2 | **Verify UPI collect requests before approving** – Always read the full description of a collect request. Legitimate services never ask for PIN entry to credit money. |
| 3 | **Do not click on shortened or suspicious payment links** – Fraudsters send fake payment links via SMS or WhatsApp. Always open your UPI app directly. |
| 4 | **Use strong, unique passwords for banking apps** – Never reuse your email or social media password for any financial application. |
| 5 | **Enable transaction alerts via SMS and email** – Instant notifications ensure you notice unauthorised transactions immediately. |
| 6 | **Never share OTPs with anyone** – Your bank, UPI provider, or government agency will NEVER ask for your OTP over the phone. |
| 7 | **Verify websites before entering payment information** – Look for `https://` and a padlock icon. Avoid making payments on websites you accessed via links in emails or messages. |
| 8 | **Lock your phone and banking apps separately** – Use biometric or PIN locks on both your device and your UPI/banking apps for double protection. |
| 9 | **Be cautious of QR code scams** – Scanning a QR code can debit money from your account. Never scan QR codes from unknown sources to "receive" money. |
| 10 | **Regularly check your bank and UPI transaction history** – Review statements weekly to catch any suspicious activity early. |

## Reporting a Cybercrime in India

If you are a victim of online fraud, act immediately:

- **National Cyber Crime Portal:** [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in) — File a complaint online 24x7
- **Cyber Crime Helpline:** **1930** (available 24x7, toll-free)
- **Nearest Police Station:** File an FIR for financial cybercrime
- **Your Bank:** Call the bank's helpline immediately to freeze transactions

> The faster you report, the higher the chance of recovering funds. Time is critical in UPI fraud cases.
